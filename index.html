Project Demo We present a descriptive, exploratory data analysis of gender representation
in the academic visualization community about papers published at
the IEEE Visualization (VIS) conference, the largest and
oldest academic visualization-focused venue.
Our work contributes to an in-depth analysis of gender
related patterns in the VIS community.

In the bubble chart displayed here, each bubble represents a paper
published in various conferences which can be distinguished by colors.
The size of the bubble indicates the citations of the paper.
The bigger the bubble the more cited the paper is which in turn infers
the more popular the paper was among the people with this field of interest
The first graph displays the analysis of overall gender representation in paper authorship over time. The second graph talks about trend for each of the child conferences that make up the IEEE visualization. The bubble chart that appears when hovered over the bars displays the top papers during that year in that conference. The size of the bubble varies according to the count of the citations.
